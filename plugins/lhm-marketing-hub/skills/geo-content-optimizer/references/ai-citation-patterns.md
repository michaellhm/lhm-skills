# AI Citation Patterns

How different AI systems select and cite content. Understanding these patterns helps optimize content for AI visibility.

## Google AI Overviews

**What gets cited**: Clear direct answers, statistics with dates, step-by-step instructions, comparison tables, definition blocks, list-formatted content.

**Structure preferences**: Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences), bullet/numbered lists, clear headings matching query intent, tables, FAQ formats.

**Authority signals**: Domain authority, E-E-A-T signals, recent publication dates, author credentials, citations to authoritative sources.

**Citation frequency**: 3-8 sources per overview.

## ChatGPT (with Browsing)

**What gets cited**: Specific facts and statistics, expert quotes, technical explanations, recent information, well-structured content.

**Source selection**: Favors .edu, .gov, .org domains. Prioritizes recognized publishers. Values comprehensive content. Combines info from multiple sources.

**Citation frequency**: 1-6 sources per response.

## Perplexity AI

**What gets cited**: Recent content (strong freshness bias), authoritative sources, quotable statements, statistical data with sources, primary sources over secondary.

**Structure preferences**: Extremely well-structured content, clear topic sentences, quotable standalone statements, factual density, headings matching question formats.

**Citation frequency**: 5-10 sources per response (most generous).

## Claude

**What gets remembered**: Clear authoritative definitions, widely-accepted facts, well-established methodologies, consensus information, content from recognized authorities.

**Content valued**: Factual accuracy and precision, logical structure, comprehensive explanations, technical accuracy, unambiguous language.

## Optimal Content Structures for Citation

1. **Definition blocks** — "[Term] is [category] that [function], [characteristic]."
2. **Statistic blocks** — "According to [Source], [statistic] as of [timeframe]."
3. **Q&A pairs** — Question heading + 40-60 word direct answer
4. **Comparison tables** — Feature rows with specific values per option
5. **Step-by-step processes** — Numbered actions with brief explanations
6. **List content** — Items with descriptions and key benefits
7. **Before/after examples** — Concrete transformation with explanation
8. **Key insight callouts** — Blockquoted memorable statements with sources

## Citation Likelihood Checklist

**High likelihood**:
- [ ] Content from recognized authority domain
- [ ] Published/updated within 12 months
- [ ] Clear, standalone statements
- [ ] Proper source attribution
- [ ] Specific statistics with dates
- [ ] Structured with headings/lists/tables

**Low likelihood**:
- Published 3+ years ago without updates
- Vague or ambiguous statements
- No sources cited
- Walls of text, poor structure
- Promotional or biased tone
